Voice-Activated Lighting System Documentation

A project to solve a lighting problem in my office

Vision

The purpose and goal of this project were to solve a pet peeve: my office light socket died, and the desk lamp casts a weird shadow on paper and my iPad when taking notes. I wanted a bright, easy-to-use voice-activated system to adjust lighting while studying.

Inspiration: I needed better, adaptable lighting for productivity and comfort.

Components Used

  • Customizable LED strip for desk lighting (adjustable brightness and colors)
  • Bluetooth modules (HC-05, one as a master, one as a slave)
  • Arduino Nanos (used for compact enclosures)
  • Solderless breadboards (for efficient wiring without permanent soldering)

Reason for choices: The components allowed flexibility and compactness. The solderless breadboards reduced wiring clutter and were perfect for the Arduino Nano's pin layout.

Assembly and Functionality

The system was built in two parts:

  1. Voice Recognition System: Configured to toggle between listening and training modes. It sends TX/RX signals to the Bluetooth master.
  2. LED Control System: Receives commands via Bluetooth slave and controls the LED strip's brightness and color.

Debugging required two laptops to verify Bluetooth pairing and command accuracy.

Code Overview: The system includes training mode, live listening, and manual command testing.

Final Outcome

The system functions perfectly at home, allowing me to control lighting with simple voice commands. While it failed in class due to ambient noise (AC), the design works as intended in a quiet environment.

Future Improvements: Upgrade the voice module to use machine learning or higher-memory Arduino boards for enhanced accuracy.
